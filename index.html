I am a final-year mechanical engineering student at NIT Srinagar with a focused interest in design-driven engineering — turning constraints into mechanisms, and mechanisms into manufacturable hardware. My work to date has centred on parametric CAD, FEA-validated structural design, and the rapid-prototyping loop that ties them together.

Through an R&D internship at Liugong India and a fabrication role at an industrial workshop, I have worked across the full design pipeline: from concept geometry in Fusion 360 and PTC Creo, through Ansys-based structural analysis, to PLM-managed release in PTC Windchill — and finally onto CNC, lathe, milling, and 3D-printed prototypes.

I am most drawn to automotive systems and mining / off-highway equipment — domains where mechanical first-principles still dominate. My goal post-graduation is to pursue advanced work and research in mechanical design, ideally within a program where simulation, materials, and manufacturability intersect.
